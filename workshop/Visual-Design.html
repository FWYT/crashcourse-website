Visual Design

By Celine Nguyen

A well-designed product is clearly understood by the user, a joy to read or use, and conveys information efficiently. How can we design to those standards? To start off, we'll talk about basic design concepts of gestalt, form, color, typography, and how to apply them. We'll also look at case studies of good and bad visual design and what we can learn from them. Resources for inspiration, advice, and best practices for common interface problems will be provided at the end.
